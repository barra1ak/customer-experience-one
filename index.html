The Customer Experience

Key Terms in Action

Customer experience has its own language. In this activity, you will explore the terms professionals use to describe customer interactions, relationships and perceptions—and then apply those terms to realistic situations.

Why These Terms Matter

A shared vocabulary helps teams create a shared experience.

Customer experience involves many departments, decisions and interactions. Understanding the terms below will help you recognize how each part of an organization can influence the customer’s overall perception.

1

Start by exploring each term. Select a card to reveal its definition and an example from a food or agricultural business setting.

01

Customer Experience

What overall impression does the customer develop?

Definition

The customer’s overall perception of an organization based on every interaction before, during and after a purchase.

Agricultural example: A producer’s impression is shaped by product quality, delivery communication, sales support and how quickly a problem is resolved.

02

Customer Service

How does the organization respond to a specific need?

Definition

The direct assistance or support an organization provides before, during or after a customer purchases a product or service.

Agricultural example: A service representative helps a customer identify a replacement part for equipment during planting season.

03

Touchpoint

Where does the customer encounter the organization?

Definition

Any point at which a customer interacts with an organization, employee, product, service or communication channel.

Agricultural example: A website visit, conversation with a sales representative, invoice, delivery and service call are all customer touchpoints.

04

Customer Journey

What path does the customer take over time?

Definition

The complete sequence of stages and interactions a customer experiences throughout a relationship with an organization.

Agricultural example: A customer researches a seed product, contacts a dealer, places an order, receives delivery and later asks for technical support.

05

Moment of Truth

Which interaction has an unusually strong impact?

Definition

A critical interaction that significantly strengthens or weakens the customer’s perception of an organization.

Agricultural example: Equipment fails during harvest, and the supplier’s response determines whether the customer feels supported or abandoned.

06

Customer Friction

What makes the experience more difficult than necessary?

Definition

Any obstacle, delay, confusion or unnecessary effort that makes it harder for a customer to accomplish a goal.

Agricultural example: A customer must call three departments before finding someone who can confirm an order’s delivery date.

07

Internal Customer

Who inside the organization depends on your work?

Definition

An employee, team or department within the organization that relies on another person or team to complete its work.

Agricultural example: The sales team relies on operations for accurate inventory information before making delivery commitments to customers.

08

Service Recovery

What happens after the organization makes a mistake?

Definition

The actions an organization takes to resolve a problem, restore confidence and rebuild the customer relationship.

Agricultural example: After sending the wrong product, the supplier acknowledges the error, arranges urgent replacement delivery and follows up afterward.

Key Takeaways

Customer experience is created one interaction at a time.

Keep these three ideas in mind as you continue through the module.

Experience Is Cumulative

Customers form opinions through the complete journey, not only through one service interaction.

Every Role Contributes

Internal communication and coordination affect what the external customer ultimately experiences.

Recovery Builds Trust

A thoughtful response to a problem can strengthen a relationship rather than simply repair a transaction.
